Research Details & Preprint
Thesis

"Sparse Exit Wave Reconstruction via Deep Unfolding"

Phase Retrieval · TEM Imaging · Deep Unfolding · Proximal Gradient Algorithm · Complex-Valued Optimization · Sparse Reconstruction · Rademacher Complexity

Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) enables high-resolution imaging of materials. The focus is on exit wave reconstruction — recovery of the complex-valued electron wave at the specimen's exit plane from intensity-only measurements — an inverse problem with a nonlinear forward model. We propose a discretized regularized variational formulation, solve it via proximal gradient algorithm (PGA) unfolded into a neural network, inspired by LISTA, enabling improved reconstruction quality and implicit dictionary learning from data.

Master's Thesis — Koopman Theory
Details
Title

"Approximation of Koopman Operators by Randomly Chosen Basis Functions"

Koopman Operators EDMD RDMD DMD Random Gaussians RINNs Spectral Analysis Metastable Regions Stochastic Differential Equations Euler–Maruyama TensorFlow Deeptime Python

Comprehensive theoretical and numerical study of the Koopman operator framework for nonlinear dynamical systems, covering spectral analysis, invariant subspaces, Koopman embedding, and data-driven approximation via EDMD.

Implementation & Results

Implemented and compared three families of basis functions — monomials up to order 10, random Gaussians, and randomly initialized neural networks (RINNs) — applied to 1D Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process and 2D double-, triple-, and quadruple-well stochastic systems. Demonstrated that random Gaussians consistently outperform monomials in approximating dominant Koopman eigenfunctions, achieving accurate metastable region identification with far fewer basis functions.
