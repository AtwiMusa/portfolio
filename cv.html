Research Details
Thesis

"Sparse Exit Wave Reconstruction via Deep Unfolding"

Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM) enables high-resolution imaging of materials, but the resulting images are difficult to interpret directly. The focus is on exit wave reconstruction — the recovery of the complex-valued electron wave at the specimen's exit plane from intensity-only measurements — an inverse problem with a nonlinear forward model. We consider a simplified forward model making the problem equivalent to phase retrieval, and propose a discretized regularized variational formulation. To solve the resulting non-convex problem, the proximal gradient algorithm (PGA) is employed and its iterations are unfolded into a neural network, where each layer corresponds to one PGA step with learnable parameters. This deep unrolling approach, inspired by LISTA, enables improved reconstruction quality, interpretability, and implicit dictionary learning from data.

Keywords: Phase Retrieval · Transmission Electron Microscopy · Deep Unfolding · Proximal Gradient Algorithm · Complex-Valued Optimization · Sparse Reconstruction · Rademacher Complexity
